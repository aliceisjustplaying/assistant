### ***Saying what you‚Äôre going to do, then doing it*** {#saying-what-you‚Äôre-going-to-do,-then-doing-it}

A newer friend once said something like ‚ÄúI like how Visa says he‚Äôs going to do something, then immediately does it.‚Äù 

This was quite funny for me to hear, because‚Ä¶ it‚Äôs actually an ADHD coping mechanism. if you ask my friends from my teenage days, they‚Äôll tell you that I was extremely unreliable, full of shit, and you simply couldn‚Äôt trust anything I said. And they were right\! This was true for me both internally and externally. I was a bullshitter by default. I would just say whatever words I needed to make things go away, and then I would forget what I had said. At some point this got me into several tangled webs that led to people getting angry and upset with me. Which was entirely my fault, and I hated myself for it.

This problem took me \*years\* to fix. And the way I ‚Äúsolved‚Äù it wasn‚Äôt pretty. My attempted solution was overkill ‚Äì I tried to impose tyrannical order on my chaotic whirlwind self. I did make some material progress, and I also made myself utterly miserable ‚Äì which I felt like I deserved, because after all, wasn‚Äôt I a lousy person?

A thing I understand intimately‚Äì which people who are still struggling are often surprised to hear, because they tend to assume that I‚Äôm a natural‚Äì is that when you‚Äôre wack, your mechanism for fixing yourself is also wack. It‚Äôs like looking for your glasses when you can‚Äôt see without your glasses. The most ‚Äúobvious‚Äù things become impossible.

The broad question of ‚Äúhow do you rebuild trust that is lost?‚Äù I had to deal with that, internally. After all, I had a long history of betraying my own trust\! There‚Äôs evidence\! So how can I trust myself? I didn‚Äôt know. I beat myself up over every failure, then wrapped that in jokes and apathy. 

I think in retrospect I‚Äôm lucky that underneath ALL of that, in the core of cores, I did still have a love for literature and music. I say earnestly that I‚Äôd give my life for musicians. I would. I think that‚Äôs the light that saved me ‚Äì the non-coercive, shining spirit of humanity.

But okay, even if I believe that there‚Äôs a light at the end of the tunnel, I still have to walk my way out. and the way to do that, when you have zero sense of balance, proprioception, muscle control, blah blah, is to put one foot in front of the other, firmly, and then do it again.

And what that looked like, for me, is announcing, ‚ÄúI‚Äôm going to drink a glass of water now,‚Äù and then drinking it. Hey, look, I just did 1x thing that I said I was going to do. ‚ÄúI‚Äôm going to do 10 pushups now.‚Äù Hey, that‚Äôs 2x things. And I earned my trust back, 1 step at a time.

This process had all sorts of second-order effects. It‚Äôs like how you might ‚Äújust‚Äù wanna play basketball with your friends, then you end up quitting cigarettes, eating healthier, sleeping better, etc, all to boost your game. And then you realize that, actually, living healthy feels great\!

Anyway, a cute vestigial remnant of this whole process is that I still announce what I‚Äôm about to do before I do it. It‚Äôs like a little ritual I have for myself. Every time I do what I say, I build trust in myself, I build self-respect (which I didn‚Äôt have until‚Ä¶ 25? 27?)

It still actually surprises me a little bit. When something I say will be done, gets done. When you‚Äôve spent a lifetime making shit up (it all started with ‚ÄúI will do my homework‚Äù), it starts to seem like magic. What I say will happen, happens? It‚Äôs like magic. I‚Äôm a magician\! üòÇ

Of course, I still make mistakes. I underestimated how long it would take me to get my ebooks published and updated. But I no longer think ‚Äúah, fuck, I‚Äôm a fucking bullshitter and nothing I say has any meaning‚Äù. I now think, ah, I made a mistake, I must recalibrate & renegotiate.

What I‚Äôm finding is that there‚Äôs a sort of ‚Äúeconomy‚Äù to it? For example, even as I write this, my ebooks are currently ‚Äúin the red‚Äù with regards to my projections. But that‚Äôs okay ‚Äì because my youtube videos are coming along beautifully. I‚Äôve been publishing 1 every single day for almost a month. Joy to the world\!

I guess this is just to say, if you feel like you don‚Äôt trust yourself, I feel you. I know how wrong it sounds when someone says ‚Äúwell, just start believing in yourself,‚Äù like b\*tch, you have evidence that you‚Äôre not to be trusted\!\!\! I know\!  The thing is to build the tiny wins.

### ***Support, don‚Äôt suppress*** {#support,-don‚Äôt-suppress}

My \#1 ‚Äúproductivity tip‚Äù for fellow weirdos and aliens with ADHD or ADHD-ish minds is ‚Äì don‚Äôt suppress your mind, support it.

At the start of every work session, write down what you intend to do.

At the end, when you have deviated wildly, don‚Äôt despair ‚Äì write down what happened.

The meandering mind is a feature, not a bug. IMO, it‚Äôs a sign that you have a stubborn curiosity within you that refuses to follow orders. This can be a great thing, actually, once you learn how to work around it. But few have the patience to teach this.

I have a pretty severe case of this myself and I used to subconsciously beat myself up over it. But over the years I‚Äôve learned to see that it takes me to interesting places. Thing is to non-judgementally go along for the ride, and report your findings.

While it‚Äôs true that there are an infinite number of things to get distracted by, it‚Äôs also simultaneously true that most things are connected ‚Äì and it‚Äôs all part of the same whole. So write it down. You‚Äôll find patterns.

Da Vinci was clearly one of us, by the way. Check out his wonderfully idiosyncratic todo list. He didn‚Äôt have Twitter, so he used notebooks. Thousands of pages of notebooks. Write. Your. Shit. Down.

The writing takes extra effort, but it builds you up tremendously. And without it, you‚Äôre left scattered, incoherent, lost.

Note-taking is the Iron Man Suit for the meandering mind.

### ***Question the assumptions*** {#question-the-assumptions}

What I've come around to see ‚Äì from having hundreds of conversations about this ‚Äì is that people make those assumptions based on what they see in any given snapshot moment. you see a kanye west daydreaming in class and you think he's "distracted"

Most people don't spend much time interrogating the lenses through which they are observing reality, and the arbitrary values encoded in the systems which they've inherited. kid can't seem to do his homework, it's almost always assumed to be the kid's fault, never the homework's

I know the other side of this, I know every side of this. There are domains in which, even outside of schools, tests, bureaucracy, etc, a lack of dexterity will hurt you. it can hurt in relationships. it can hurt in trying to keep your word, to yourself and people you care about

one way in which I describe this is that ADHD minds are like big, lumbersome chainguns that have a tremendous rate of fire. but they take time to spin up. they jam easily. they are no more or less worthy than any other kind of mind

big part of the challenge of having a neurodivergent mind is understanding how it is different, what are the contexts in which it excels, what are the contexts in which it doesn't, how to seek help/assistance for what you're not good at, and so on

### ***Put the gun down*** {#put-the-gun-down}

Somebody once tweeted, ‚ÄúADHD is a lot of mentally yelling at yourself to DO something to do ANYTHING while you sit with absolutely no expression on your face, scrolling through your phone or continuing to play a game or whatever as if you have no control over your body.‚Äù

With love: in my view, this isn‚Äôt ADHD itself, but punitive, coercive self-bullying. It‚Äôs likely inherited from the people in your life who didn‚Äôt know how to deal with your ADHD.

This is \*mismanagement\* of ADHD (it‚Äôs not your fault if you don‚Äôt know better), not ADHD itself.

Yelling at people is a horrible strategy for behaviour modification. this is true internally as well. it typically comes from a place of desperation and neediness and it almost always makes things worse rather than better.

if you‚Äôre at a point where going about your daily life is the equivalent of having someone screaming in your face, things have gotten pretty dire. It's salvageable but it will take time. you need to rehabilitate your relationship with yourself. You start by putting the gun down.

![][image3]

‚ÄúDO something do ANYTHING‚Äù is a really bad request to ask of anyone, by the way. (Again I say this with love.)

It‚Äôs a good sign, actually, because it suggests to me that you can improve your relationship by taking some simple steps to [get better at asking yourself for help](http://visakanv.com/blog/help/).

Where ADHD \*does\* come into the picture: In my experience with myself and dozens of others, ADHD is the slipperiest, most irreverent fucker on Earth üòÇ which is to say he/she/they will resist your coercion and bullying TO THE DEATH.

you cannot win this fight. put the gun down.

I repeat: your ADHD is smarter than you, more stubborn than you, and WILL outmaneuver you.

The ADHD guerrillas in your head will outlast the authoritarian govt of your executive function every time, however repressive you get.

You cannot win this fight. Put the gun down.

‚ÄúNo but maybe if I yell harder, hold myself hostage, plead harder, beat myself to within an inch of my life, the strategy that has never worked in my life will suddenly work\! I must self-flagellate harder\!‚Äù

No. It does not work. You cannot win this fight. Put the gun down.

![][image4]  
‚ÄúOkay\! Okay\! I‚Äôll put the gun down. But now what? Nothing works in this relationship. I can‚Äôt be trusted, I am a bad naughty person who just wants to avoid responsibility and hardship, a loser, a piece of shit‚Ä¶‚Äù

Breathe. It might not seem like it but all of this is fixable.

At this stage in your relationship (with yourself) your claims and beliefs about yourself have all been warped by neediness, anger, frustration, insecurity. They might be superficially correct in some way, but they are noxious, demeaning, dispiriting. We can fix this

you have to declare a trust bankruptcy. a resentment jubilee. recognise that the approach so far has not worked and that you have the equivalent of a psychic injury that needs treatment and rehabilitation. There is no shame in this.

Trust is rebuilt with small baby steps. you have to ask yourself very small, achievable things. ask yourself for a glass of water. go do that. drink. see? you just rebuilt a tiny bit of trust in yourself. That's a win. That's proof that you CAN be trusted. baby steps. baby steps.  
You can do it. I believe in you, even if you don‚Äôt yet believe in yourself. You have within yourself a strength and power that you don‚Äôt even know, that has been forgotten. Dark have been your dreams of late. But [I come to you now at the turn of the tide](https://twitter.com/visakanv/status/1289175852379324417).

I invite you to see the best in yourself. Here's a story:

### ***You can‚Äôt intimidate ADHD into submission*** {#you-can‚Äôt-intimidate-adhd-into-submission}

*Caveat: all of the following is my personal experience. Your mileage may vary.*

ADHD is slippery and resists negative reinforcement. You can‚Äôt frighten/intimidate/bully ADHD away. I tried. For years. It doesn‚Äôt work. ADHD is very, very, very stubborn.  And maybe rightfully so. You can‚Äôt ‚Äúcontain‚Äù it because it will outlast your executive function. ADHD can remain ‚Äúirrational‚Äù longer than your executive function can stay solvent (h/t @rplevy).

This is something people really don‚Äôt seem to get so I think it‚Äôs worth repeating: the game is rigged. No amount of clever strategizing can really help you outwit an opponent who is stronger, faster, and just better than you on every dimension. I struggled & failed until I accepted this.

There *is* a lot of work that a person with ADHD can do to make life less miserable. But what I‚Äôm trying to convey here is that you can‚Äôt install an authoritarian government and expect to control a messy, chaotic, irreverent, independent people. You have to respect their autonomy.

I have a good enough relationship with myself now that I can joke about it. But there is a truth here:

![][image6]  
 Not all people respond to repression the same way. Some will literally choose death. There is staggering variance here that many people don‚Äôt seem to appreciate

If I am a charming and persuasive person, maybe half of it is that I had to charm and persuade others to survive socially, but another half of it is that I have to charm and persuade myself to get me to do \*anything\*. It‚Äôs a tremendous amount of work. It did pay off eventually, but it took a lot of work over a long time.

I am [really like this](https://twitter.com/visakanv/status/1074979348749312001) even today, and it has gotten in the way of my life ‚Äî so much so that I‚Äôve basically designed my entire life around this

I can recognise that it‚Äôs unhelpful/costly and try to admonish etc, but ADHD brain does not give a fuck about negative reinforcement.

I do recognise that there‚Äôs a flavor of defeatism in some people‚Äôs tweets about their ADHD, and I don‚Äôt encourage that. But I understand it. Sometimes it‚Äôs a coping mechanism, sometimes it can be something much worse. Which is why I really like the frame ‚Äúconstructive ADHD‚Äù.

### ***Trickster spirit*** {#trickster-spirit}

Kids in my DMs with ADHD symptoms are always surprised when I tell them that I don't think they should try to suppress their mind's wild swings. My advice is to develop the practice of taking meticulous notes instead. It worked for many prolific individuals throughout history.

To have ADHD, in my view, is to be blessed & cursed to be the custodian of a wild trickster spirit who refuses to be tamed, broken, refuses to obey anybody else's directions ‚Äì including your own. You can't win, IMO, so you might as well accept your fate and go along for the ride

![][image7]

Which isn't to say that you don't have to care about your worldly responsibilities. you do. That's the curse part. the trickster doesn't cooperate, or live on your schedule, but you have to clean up their messes. Accepting this can be painful; that's what growing up is. But if you take care of your shit, if you don't get grumpy and upset and grovel, if you don't resent the wild child inside you ‚Äì then you get to experience the blessing. and the blessing is that they will lead you on the grandest adventures that other people can't even imagine

Don't beat yourself up, don't thrash about ‚Äì that'll likely make it worse. try to relax. try to believe. you might be different from all your peers, but you are not alone. You are not broken or spoiled. Others have been on this journey before, and they are some of humanity's best.

### ***Mind palace*** {#mind-palace}

I‚Äôve basically taught myself to manage my ADHD with notes and threads. My ‚Äúschedule intelligence‚Äù (deadlines, calendars, checklist) is terrible but my recognition and web-jumping is fantastic, so I spent something like a decade using the latter to build an elaborate mind-palace.

Because of threading and recognition, I can almost always pick up where I left off.

I used to feel guilty and ashamed about the idea of having to rely on a prosthetic, but I‚Äôve since learned to see it as a mecha for the mind, like an iron man suit. (next section)

If you‚Äôre like me, I recommend keeping meticulous notes of whatever you do when you‚Äôre procrastinating. I think of it as ‚Äúdeep-self-directed work‚Äù. If you‚Äôre going to watch trashy movies, then write down your thoughts after watching each movie. It‚Äôll come in handy in ways you least expect.

To me, the most critical part of becoming ‚Äúso good they can‚Äôt ignore you‚Äù (h/t Cal Newport, Steve Martin) is to be ‚Äúso prolific you don‚Äôt recognise yourself‚Äù. Once you cross that threshold you can actually look at your own work with a relatively objective, critical eye.

People are sometimes surprised to hear how agnostic and indifferent I am to specific methods, formats, tools. The only thing that really matters to me is a sense of flow and throughput through the entire pipeline.

Getting lost is a feature not a bug, the only real problem is getting jammed.

### ***Some random note-taking advice*** {#some-random-note-taking-advice}

Someone recently asked me for note-taking advice via DM, and I‚Äôm still thinking about what I told them because I think I accidentally stumbled upon a simple formulation that makes a lot of sense to me

Start with one notepad file, or one paper journal, whatever. Don‚Äôt think about topics, don‚Äôt think about how you‚Äôre going to sort it, what the sub-folders should be, etc. Just start dumping all your thoughts. Use line breaks, make it ‚Äúchunky‚Äù (as opposed to long paragraphs)

Keep going until you \*begin to feel overwhelmed\*. Now pause. Your notepad file is now full of many discrete (as opposed to continuous) points, too many for you to remember or make sense of at a glance. Great\! Now it‚Äôs time to sort.

You now want to look for connections \*between\* nodes. Resist the urge to impose some sort of top-down grand scheme. Instead, look for related ideas, riffs, ‚Äúthings that go together‚Äù. ‚ÄúSiblings‚Äù. Here‚Äôs an eg of how I‚Äôm doing this retroactively on my blog

You see what‚Äôs happening? Dozens (100s if you nasty like me) of ‚Äúatoms‚Äù start to coalesce into a smaller handful of ‚Äúmolecules‚Äù. You no longer need to consider and recall every single thing. You just need to remember the core molecules, and recognize ‚Äúwhat goes with what‚Äù

### ***My style*** {#my-style}

quick sketch of my personal (ideal) style: 

move fast, hit hard, wipe quick and move on to the next thing, but be micro-rigorous in making sure that each new thing quickly considers all past things, and is threaded accordingly.

my biggest weakness at the moment is actually "wipe quick" ‚Äì I sit with half-done things for too long, but the real problem isn't that they're half-done, but that they're not properly threaded

properly-threaded half-dones are \*fine\*. lingering on a half-done for too long is not.

by "properly threaded" I mean contextualized against everything else that I'm doing, plotted on the (multi-thread, lol) map of my body of work. The act of contextualizing a thing ‚Äì saying what it‚Äôs for, what it was trying to do, etc ‚Äì makes it useful even if it‚Äôs incomplete.

### ***Go fast and get it all down*** {#go-fast-and-get-it-all-down}

if you think "oh gosh I can't slow down, my mind goes a million miles a minute", put that mf  
 to work. write down every word, or record yourself speaking. can you write or talk for 8 hours straight? have you done it? do you have a record of it?

(generalizing from dozens of DMs over the years)
